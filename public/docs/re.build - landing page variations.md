# **VARIATION 1: FOMO & URGENCY**

## **Strategic Thinking**

**Core Psychology:** Loss aversion‚Äîpeople work harder to avoid losing something than to gain something equal.

**Hypothesis:** Emphasizing scarcity and time pressure will increase application rate by pushing visitors to act now rather than "bookmark for later."

**Key Changes from Current:**

* Harder deadlines and countdown language  
* Explicit "what you lose" if you wait  
* More aggressive spot-tracking  
* Before/after positioning (founding vs. standard terms)

**Best Tested On:** Paid traffic (Google/Meta ads), returning visitors

**Risk:** Can feel manipulative. May attract lower-quality leads who respond to pressure rather than fit.

# **VARIATION 2: RESULTS & SOCIAL PROOF**

## **Strategic Thinking**

**Core Psychology:** Social proof‚Äîpeople trust evidence over promises, and they trust peer experiences over company claims.

**Hypothesis:** Leading with specific, measurable outcomes and concrete details builds credibility faster than emotional appeals. Numbers feel trustworthy.

**Key Changes from Current:**

* Metrics and data throughout  
* Case study / project details  
* Process transparency that demonstrates expertise  
* Third-party validation signals  
* "Show, don't tell" approach

**Best Tested On:** Organic search traffic, referrals, comparison shoppers

**Risk:** May feel less emotionally engaging. Need enough proof to back claims.

**Note:** Since you're still building portfolio, some "proof" will be process-focused and forward-looking. Update with real metrics as projects complete.
